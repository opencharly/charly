# H part 2a — cdp resolves its endpoint via the generic reverse-leg (delete the host cdp preresolver)

First of the endpoint-verb migrations (plan's H ↳ row): the `cdp` verb no longer has a
host-side preresolver shipping a pre-built DevTools URL in CheckEnv.Substrate. The
out-of-process candy/plugin-cdp resolves its own dialable endpoint via the new generic
`cc.ResolveEndpoint` reverse-leg.

## What moved

- Host: `resolveVerbEndpoint(port)` (check_endpoint_resolve.go) — the ONE generic
  venue→host-reachable-addr resolution (resolveCheckVenue + resolveCheckEndpoint), serving
  BOTH the in-process `runnerCheckContext.ResolveEndpoint` and the out-of-process
  `checkContextReverseServer.ResolveEndpoint`. Forwards it opens are tracked per-Invoke on the
  Runner and drained by `invokeVerbProvider` after the Invoke (the forward must outlive the
  plugin's dial — the flagged forward-lifetime, solved by the per-Invoke `cc`).
- Plugin: `candy/plugin-cdp` builds its CheckContext via `sdk.NewCheckContext` and calls
  `cc.ResolveEndpoint(9222)`, then `http://<addr>` — keeping its own `sdk.VerbVerdict`
  matcher pipeline (no kit-verb conversion needed; a raw provider reaches the reverse channel).
- Deleted (R5): the host cdp preresolver (`preresolveCdpEndpoint`, `CdpEnv`, `cdpDevTools`) +
  its `verbPreresolvers` registry entry; stale doc refs swept (vnc/spice/check_cmd). Only the
  `devToolsTab` /json struct (status_probes.go) remains host-side.
